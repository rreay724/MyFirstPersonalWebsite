Hello there! My name is Bobby Reay and I'm a Software/Web Developer. I was a Technical Recruiter for 13 years before transitioning to Development and I couldn't be happier.

In 2017, I was working on filling a position for a Linux Systems Administrator who had an active Full Scope Polygraph clearance. Needless to say, these candidates are extremely hard to find. After days of searching, I came across a candidate who was the perfect fit. I noticed he didn't have a degree, only a Red Hat certification, which told me that he has a passion for this, and if he learned it on his own, he must be very driven. I submitted his resume to the manager and within a couple days he was in for an interview - he nailed it. The manager told me to make him an offer of 115k, so I got on the phone immediately to break the good news. His reply; "Heh, I'm actually making 170k right now"."Sorry, did you say One One Seven or One Seven Zero?". "One Seven Zero". As you may have guessed, we couldn't afford him, and the first thing that popped in my head was "I'm in the wrong business". I had attended a "Tech School" in 2001 where I used Linux, so I knew I could do it. I immediately signed up for LinuxAcademy.com and started studying for my Linux+ certification in hopes of becoming a Linux Systems Administrator myself. I started studying from ground zero in May 2017, and in October 2017 I received my Linux+ certification.

After receiving my Linux+ I started working with a couple managers at work to get some hands on experience on some of the programs we had while I worked towards my Network+ next. After a few weeks of it, I realized my heart was just not into the networking side of things. I kept studying anyways, and in April 2018 I built my first PC. A coworker of mine was working on a position for a Mobile Developer and was having an extremely hard time finding someone. She recommended that I learn mobile development because it seemed like they were in high demand. My initial thought - "I don't have the brain for that. There's no way I could learn how to code." That night, I went home to my newly built PC I put together a week prior, downloaded Android Studio and bought a course on Udemy to see how coding fit me. I chose Android development specifically so I could make things visually while also learning Java. I instantly fell in love with it. I studied every day learning as much as I could as fast as I could.

A few months later in December 2018, I went on a work trip to St. Louis to help with recruiting candidates for positions we had with a government agency out there. Along on that trip was one of the lead developers on our IRAD team. We all went to dinner and I talked to her about coding and what I've been learning and how much I love it.Fast forward a few weeks and I was offered a position as a Software Developer on their team rebuilding a federal government agency application from the ground up. It was backend development using Java and Spring Boot. I couldn't believe it - my life just changed forever and it would have never happened if I didn't meet that one person on the work trip who gave me a chance.
After a year, I had fulfilled my duties on that specific program and was moved on to the IRAD team to work on other projects, which is where I discovered UI and Web Development, and realized it's a much better fit than only doing backend work. I enjoy backend work, but even more than that, I enjoy making visual projects.

If you've stuck with me this long reading through my About Me, it is greatly appreciated and here is a little easter egg I added to the site that you may enjoy (volume up). My passion for coding and hard work learning it brought me so much happiness and fulfillment in life. If you have any interest in coding, I hope my story inspires you to follow it and know that if I can do it, so can you.
